# The Creative Act: A Way of Being

Rick Rubin's meditation on creativity as a universal practice, not reserved for artists. The book reads more like philosophy than a how-to guide.

## Takeaways

- Creativity is about paying attention, not about talent
- The work exists before you make it — your job is to receive it
- Constraints and limitations are creative fuel, not obstacles

## Sparked

- [[notes/iteration-over-perfection|Iteration over perfection]] — Rubin's emphasis on making many things quickly
- The idea that attention itself is a creative act connects to daily journaling practice
