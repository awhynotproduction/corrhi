# Iteration Over Perfection

The best creative work comes from making many versions quickly, not from perfecting one attempt. A bad first draft you can see and react to is worth more than a perfect draft that only exists in your head.

This is why [[notes/constraint-as-freedom|constraints help creativity]] — they force you to commit to a version instead of endlessly optimizing. Time constraints are especially powerful: "make something in 30 minutes" produces more insight than "make something perfect."

The [[projects/community-garden|community garden project]] works this way — plant things, see what grows, iterate. Don't design the perfect garden on paper first.

Related to [[notes/shared-meals-as-ritual|shared meals]] in that both prioritize the act of doing over the quality of the output. A mediocre dinner you actually cook and share is better than a perfect recipe you never attempt.
